One Car Can't See Everything

Buildings, trucks, and sharp corners create blind spots that even the best sensors can't overcome — unless cars team up and share what they each see.

Without Collaboration

The Blind Spot Problem

Every self-driving car has a limited field of view. A large truck, a building at an intersection, or a curve in the road can completely hide pedestrians or other vehicles until it's too late.

Traditional solutions tried transmitting enormous amounts of raw sensor data between cars — but that required bandwidth that doesn't exist on real roads.

Other approaches shared simplified 2D maps of the scene, losing the crucial 3D detail needed to understand what's actually there and how tall it is.

⚡ Up to 48.5% of driving scenarios involve occluded objects
